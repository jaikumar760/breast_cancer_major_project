Support & Resources

Find help and guidance through these trusted Indian and global breast cancer support resources.

Indian Helplines

  • Indian Cancer Society: 1800-22-1951 (Toll-free)
  • Cancer Patients Aid Association: +91-22-24924000
  • National Cancer Helpline: 011-22901701 (Delhi)
  • Women's Cancer Initiative - Tata Memorial: 022-24177000

Specialized Hospitals

  • Tata Memorial Hospital, Mumbai: tmc.gov.in
  • All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), Delhi: aiims.edu
  • Rajiv Gandhi Cancer Institute, Delhi: rgci.org
  • Adyar Cancer Institute, Chennai: cancerinstitutewia.in

NGOs & Support Groups

  • CanSupport Delhi: cansupport.org
  • Cancer Foundation of India: cancerfoundationofindia.org
  • Uday Foundation: udayfoundation.org
  • Indian Breast Cancer Survivors Network: Facebook support group

Government Schemes

  • Ayushman Bharat Yojana: Coverage for cancer treatment
  • Health Minister's Cancer Patient Fund: Financial assistance
  • State Health Missions: Varies by state - check local hospitals
  • National Cancer Grid: Connects cancer centers nationwide

Global Helplines

  • WHO Cancer Program: who.int/cancer
  • American Cancer Society: 1-800-227-2345
  • Breast Cancer Now (UK): 0808 800 6000
  • Cancer Council Australia: 13 11 20

International Organizations

  • Breast Cancer Research Foundation: bcrf.org
  • Susan G. Komen: komen.org
  • Union for International Cancer Control: uicc.org

Research & Clinical Trials

  • ClinicalTrials.gov: Find trials worldwide
  • International Cancer Genome Consortium: icgc.org

Emergency Contacts (India): Dial 108 for ambulance or visit nearest government hospital

Breast Cancer Awareness in India

  • October is observed as Breast Cancer Awareness Month
  • Mumbai, Delhi, and Chennai have the highest incidence rates
  • Early detection through mammography available at most district hospitals
  • Pink Ribbon Campaigns organized nationwide during October
